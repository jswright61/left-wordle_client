Privacy Policy

What Left Wordle collects, why, and who else ever sees it. Last updated 2026-07-13.

Short version
Left Wordle is a hobby project, not a company collecting data to sell. Your game history and stats live in your browser unless you create a Passkey account. An account needs no name, email, or password to exist. We don't run ads, don't use ad trackers, and don't sell or share your data with anyone.

1.What's stored on your device

By default, everything about how you play — your guesses, win/loss history, streaks, and preferences (dark mode, hard mode, etc.) — is stored only in your browser's local storage. None of it reaches our servers unless you create an account (see below). Clearing your browser data or switching devices loses this local history unless you've created an account or used the backup tools in Troubleshooting.

2.What's sent to our servers just by playing

Each time you play, your browser generates a random device ID (not tied to your name or any account) and we record it alongside the puzzle date, your guesses, and a coarse country code (from Cloudflare's edge network, not your raw IP address — we never store your IP). This is how we measure things like daily players and where the game is used, in aggregate. It isn't linked to an identity unless you're logged in on that device, at which point it's associated with your account like the rest of your history.

3.Accounts & Passkeys

See Logins & Passkeys for the full explanation. In short: accounts use passkeys, not usernames or passwords, and need no personal information to create. An account is a randomly generated ID plus whatever passkeys you've registered to it. If you set an email, it's used only to deliver one-time links for adding a device or recovering account access — never for login itself, and never for marketing or anything else.

Once you're logged in, your history, stats, preferences, and current game sync to our server under your account so they follow you across devices. That data is tied only to your account ID and optional email — nothing else about you is collected.

4.Cookies

If you're logged in, we set an HttpOnly session cookie (invisible to any JavaScript, including ours) that keeps you signed in. During a passkey registration or login attempt, a short-lived signed cookie holds the in-progress challenge for a few minutes. Neither cookie is used for advertising or cross-site tracking, and we don't set any third-party ad or tracking cookies.

5.Third parties involved

ServiceWhat it's for
Fastmail Sends the one-time device-link and account-recovery emails, and the optional diagnostics email below. Doesn't receive anything else.
Sentry Crash/error reporting on the live site only — receives stack traces and browser info when something breaks, not your game data or identity.
Cloudflare Fronts the site and supplies the coarse country code used in section 2. Standard infrastructure for any site behind it.

6."Send Settings to Developers"

The Troubleshooting section has an opt-in button that emails a copy of your local data (and, if you're logged in, your account email, preferences, stats, and history) directly to the developer, to help debug an issue you're reporting. This only ever happens if you click that button yourself — nothing is sent automatically.

7.Deleting your data

You can disable individual passkeys yourself from the account menu's Manage Passkeys screen. To delete an account and all associated data entirely, email scott@left-wordle.com and we'll remove it.

8.Changes to this policy

If what we collect changes, this page will be updated to reflect it.

9.Contact

Questions about any of this: scott@left-wordle.com.
